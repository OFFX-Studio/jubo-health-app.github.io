What it is

A gamified wellness app: a 10-second AI face scan (plus your health data) keeps a living aquarium of collectible creatures healthy, led by Bobo. Families co-raise one tank.

Why it wins

A familiar, long-life theme even an 80-year-old gets; gentle, non-coercive design; a procedural-art content engine; and Jubo's proprietary AI measurement + channel as an unfair advantage.

Free core → cosmetics → Viva+ subscription. A real consumer business that recoups its build in year one, cosmetics on top.

Jubo's data + channel is the moat: consumer signals feed Jubo AI and funnel families into B2B. And OFFX Studio brings 10+ years of award-winning, gamified consumer-app craft to build it.

The name

Why "Viva"

Viva means "live!" — a celebration of being alive. It names exactly what the app is about: your real vitality keeps a whole little world alive, and it's built to keep you thriving over a long, long life.

Alive

From the Latin for "to live." Your heartbeat and daily vitals literally keep the tank alive — show up, and it comes to life.

Vitality

Viva shares a root with vital and vivid — energy, health and brightness. A healthier you makes a brighter, thriving tank.

Long life

"Viva!" is a cheer for long life. Creatures never die, and koi and turtles carry longevity meaning — a gentle promise the app keeps.

Why gamification

In the AI era, features are commodities — experience is the moat

A scan, a tracker, a chart — any single function can be cloned, or absorbed by an AI assistant or platform, almost overnight. A pure-utility health app has no defensibility. What can't be copied is an experience people are attached to and return to every day: a habit, a creature they've raised, a family ritual built over years.

✕ A pure utility

Scan-and-leave. Easily replaced the moment something cheaper, faster or AI-native appears. Scanners churn — there's no reason to stay.

✓ An experience

A living tank you tend, creatures you've collected, a family you check in with. Emotional investment compounds — and is almost impossible to replicate.

🦉

Duolingo is the proof. Learning a language is a "utility" — Google Translate and ChatGPT can teach you for free. Yet Duolingo is a multi-billion-dollar company, because the gamified experience (streaks, the owl, the daily ritual) is what people can't leave. The lesson is commodity; the experience is the moat.

💛

And it's a bridge between people. A shared creature and tank is a gentle intermediary between family and friends — a warm, low-pressure reason to reach out, especially for elders, without the awkwardness of a cold message. The game carries the care.

Viva puts the AI measurement utility inside an experience. The scan is the hook; the tank, the creatures and co-raising are the moat. A competitor can copy a face scan overnight — they can't copy the years a family has spent raising their tank together.

Social, novelty and fun are still what make people try a new app — and gamification is still the element that keeps them coming back. In an AI era where features are quickly commoditized, a scanner can only compete on price; a little world you've raised with your family can't be replaced. That is the moat.

Why an aquarium is the perfect theme

Two reasons it fits better than anything else. First, it maps naturally onto the body: clear, calm water is exactly the calm the face scan measures — water quality becomes a legible read-out of your health, understood at a glance. Second, it's a deeply shared experience in Asia — fish tanks fill homes, shops and restaurants; koi and turtles symbolize longevity and good fortune; and keeping or watching fish is a cross-generational memory an 80-year-old needs zero learning to enjoy. (Watching fish is also clinically shown to lower stress and blood pressure.)

What keeps it healthy, not manipulative

The same mechanics that drive retention can tip into harm. These are the lines the design holds.

✕ Not Candy Crush

No engineered frustration. Measurement earns energy, never gates it. You're never locked out of your own health data to force a return.

✕ Not a companion trap

The AI stays deliberately tiny: a reactive creature, not an attachment-maximizing chatbot. That keeps it clear of the regulatory heat around companion apps.

✓ Pikmin-gentle

Passive accrual and no punishment for lapses. Miss a day and the water clouds gently, then clears. The creatures never die, and they wait patiently.

✓ Wellness, not medical

AI readings are framed as screening-grade trend-tracking with cuff confirmation: a consumer product, never a diagnostic device.

The business model

A consumer business in its own right

Jubo Viva stands on proven free-to-play wellness economics: a large free base, gentle cosmetic and subscription revenue, and content partnerships. It's built to be a real business. And because it runs on Jubo's data and reaches Jubo's future customers, it does something a standalone app never could: it compounds the B2B core underneath.

Revenue How the app makes money

Freemium wellness-game economics: a big free base, a small share converting into cosmetics and Viva+, with partnerships layered on. Health data is never paywalled; monetization sits entirely in delight and depth.
